CAD

The first step in 3D printing is CAD. CAD is where you design the 3D model of the object that you want to print. I personally use Fusion360, which is free if you don't use it to make money, although there are many other programs from which you can design 3D models, such as Blender which is free.

Printer Types

FDM is the most popular and well known type of 3D printing, it makes objects by applying layer after layer of heated plastic that is extruded in a thin stream from a nozzle. This is the cheapest and most common type of 3D printing, and filaments for it are widely available. It does however, have a slow print speed, and it is limited in precision due to the minimum width of the nozzle and filament. Popular 3D Printer

Filament Types

There are many types of filaments for FDM 3D printing, in many types of plastics and colors. Filament comes on a spool and is unwound and melted by the printer, and extruded. The most common, and easiest to work with is PLA (polylactic acid). PLA comes in many colors, works in a fairly wide range of temperatures, is durable and strong, although not very flexible.
